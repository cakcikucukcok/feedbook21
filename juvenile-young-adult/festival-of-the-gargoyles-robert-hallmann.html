It is the 18th century and the Thames marshes are a dangerous place for children. This is a tale of friendship in a changing world, where the smugglers and criminals still have the upper hand on the community. Yet it is Lizzie, a feisty 10-year-old girl, who is the strongest of all with her amazing spirit of survival, overcoming her fear of the gargoyles to resuce the people from a fire. Her prize is an education in a time when sending girls to school was considered a waste. She becomes the target of many but she has a most unusual hero, a mute gypsy boy, and together they once again rescue the villagers from terror, although it is all their own making. Hot off the press and the reviews are already proving this is a children's novel to notice: "The characters and scenes leap out of the pages through detailed description and beautiful sketched images, that provide a new depth to the story. Courageous Lizzie, scary villain smuggler Sam Smagge and supportive friend Stefan enable children to truly picture these fascinating characters. A must read for children."
