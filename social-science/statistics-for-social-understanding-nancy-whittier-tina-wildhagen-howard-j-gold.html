Statistics for Social Understanding: With Stata and SPSS introduces students to the way statistics is used in the social sciences: as a tool for advancing understanding of the social world. It is designed for introductory students in the social sciences, including those with little quantitative background, as it covers the conceptual aspects of statistics in detail even when the mathematical details are minimized. With coverage of statistical topics, a balanced approach to calculation, and directions on how to use statistical software, data is analyzed to explore and answer exciting questions. To accommodate changing undergraduate courses, the text incorporates examples from both Stata and SPSS. Written in a clear and engaging style, Statistics for Social Understanding provides practice problems of every type as well as easily available datasets for classroom use.

Features Include:

  • Stata and SPSS explanations and examples in every chapter.
  • Statistics analysis taught in the way it is used in the social sciences.
  • Real world applications integrated throughout the chapters.
  • Research on the teaching and learning of quantitative material incorporated into the content.
  • Multiple types of practice problems provided both in the text and on the open access Companion Website.
  • Instructor and student ancillaries written by the authors.
